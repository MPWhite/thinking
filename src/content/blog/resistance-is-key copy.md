Endurance training has played a major role in my life for >50% of the last 30 years. 

When I was 12 I raced my first Olympic Distance triathlon (1500m open water swim, 24.2 mile bike, 6.2 mile run). My father thought the event might toughen me up, so much to the concern of my mother and fellow racers, I lined up for the 2007 Festival of Flowers triathlon in Greenwood South Carolina. 

I'm not sure that the experience taught my anything, but I do remember feeling quite proud at the end. So proud, that I announced my intention to skip all the way to the Ironman distance (2.4 mile swim, 112 mile bike, 26.2 mile run). The Ironman governing body obviously wasn't too keen on a 12 year old running the event, so the goal had to wait a few years.
